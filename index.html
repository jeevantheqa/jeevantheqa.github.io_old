Summer Project : Error Correction and Coding

BCH Codes

  • BCH (Bose, Chaudhuri, and Hocquenghem) codes are block based cyclic error correcting codes which are used to correct multiple error correction patterns. BCH codes are based on Galois fields or finite fields with a particularly chosen generator polynomial.

Reed Solomon(RS)

  • Reed Solomon(RS) Codes are a subset of BCH codes and are linear block codes. The RS encoder works similar to BCH one, it takes k data symbols of s bits each and adds parity symbols to make an n symbol codeword.

Details of Research

  • In our summer internship, we worked on the simulation of BCH codes and RS codes using MATLAB. Our aim was to understand the working of how the error corrections of codes are performed during transfer of messages in binary form. We concentrated on the BCH codes and Reed-Solomon codes, working of their encoder and decoder methods and the results after the decoding is performed. Initially we worked with BCH codes, starting from the root we implemented basic but most commonly used form of the code . On working of its simulation we checked the performance of the respective code. Then for the RS code we performed the same task of generating a codec, encoding it adding noise to the encoded code. Then we decoded the code and checked for error and its performance.
